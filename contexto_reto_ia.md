# "Drone Digital Twin" Challenge Context

The project consists of a digital twin of the kinematics and dynamics of a drone that will be mounted on a test stand (FFT GYRO). On this test stand, through a COM port, we will be able to collect roll, pitch, and yaw angles (in addition to those I will obtain from the Pixhawk flight controller). These angle measurements, along with calculations (I propose taking the formulas from "Modelling and control of quadcopter" by Teppo Luukkonen), must be converted into $X$, $Y$, $Z$ movement in the digital twin.

Below, I am sharing the email left by the advisor in charge of the project idea:

> I am sharing a more direct response regarding each point:
>
> **Physical drone rotation data: Roll, Pitch, and Yaw from the FFT GYRO**
> These values will be used primarily to validate the digital model. That is, the digital twin should estimate those angles by itself, and then you will compare those results against the real values measured by the FFT GYRO to see how well the rotational dynamics are modeled.
>
> **Digital twin controller**
> It is not necessary for the digital twin to include a complete flight controller of its own. It is most convenient to use the signals going to the motors/ESCs as input, not just the original stick signal.
>
> The idea is that, from those signals, the model can estimate all the drone's state variables:
> - $X$, $Y$, $Z$
> - Roll, Pitch, Yaw
>
> Of all these, in this setup, we will only be able to directly measure Roll, Pitch, and Yaw.
>
> **Input signals to the twin**
> If you are going to use the signals to the ESCs as input, then it will be very important that you characterize the relationship between the ESC signal and the thrust generated by each motor/propeller.
> You will also have to estimate the mass and inertia of the drone. Furthermore, remember that the measured dynamics do not depend solely on the drone, but also on the inertia added by the FFT GYRO itself and the mounting system, so that must also be considered within the model.
>
> **Real-time**
> The idea is that the digital twin can run in real-time or near real-time. I wouldn't want the project to be limited only to post-processing.
>
> **$X$, $Y$, and $Z$ Validation**
> The final objective is indeed for the digital twin to have the ability to move in $X$, $Y$, and $Z$, even if the real drone attached to the FFT GYRO cannot physically do so.
> However, since those variables cannot be measured directly at this stage, it will not be possible to validate them experimentally for now.
> Therefore, in this phase, we are going to focus on validating the model with what we *can* measure:
> - Roll
> - Pitch
> - Yaw
>
> If the mathematical model is well-built and the Roll, Pitch, and Yaw estimates are close to the real FFT GYRO measurements, then we can say that the drone's dynamics are reasonably well modeled.
>
> In summary, the general idea would be this:
>
> *Input to the digital twin*: 
> - Signals to motors/ESCs
>
> *States the model must estimate*:
> - $X$, $Y$, $Z$ 
> - Roll, Pitch, Yaw
>
> *Available variables for validation*: 
> - Roll, Pitch, Yaw
>
> *Main goal at this stage*: 
> - Properly validate the rotational part of the model
>
> *Additional goal*:
> - Run the system in real-time or near real-time
>
> *Out of scope for this phase*: 
> - Directly validate $X$, $Y$, and $Z$
>
> I hope this helps clarify the project's direction better.
>
> I remain available for any questions or comments.

With what was stated in the advisor's email, I understand that the main goal is to see the $X$, $Y$, $Z$ movement of the drone model within the digital twin.

Currently, we are running motor tests to eventually extract the variables $T$ and $k$ (Thrust in the body direction and the thrust constant), and modeling the drone in SolidWorks to obtain an inertia matrix similar to the real one so it can serve as $M$.
